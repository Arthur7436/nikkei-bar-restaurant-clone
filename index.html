The name Nikkei refers to people of Japanese ancestry living abroad as citizens of other countries. It also specifically refers to the cuisine of the Japanese-Peruvians which started more than a century ago when the first Japanese immigrated to Peru in the late 1890s. Combining Japanese techniques and flavours with local Peruvian produce, a diverse and unique cuisine was born.

Our menu features Nikkoi-styled share plates alongside a 40-strong wine list and Peruvian-Japanese inspired cocktails. We look forward to welcoming you (back!) to Nikkoi.

“Nikkoi cuisine shows so much freedom in the kitchen. There are no barriers with the coming together of Peruvian and Japanese traditions and we hope to bring a little excitement. Welcome to our home.”
